Tamales

Description

Tamales are a delicious traditional Mexican dish made from a corn based dough mixture. They are always filled with various things such as chili or meat, because of which there exits a lot of recipes for them. Some examples of different types of tamales include: rajas, mole, and green chili. Also corn, yes more corn.

This recipe will follow the most popular variant of tamales, red chili with pork. Nothing quite beats freshly steamed red chili tamale with a side of champurrado and bunuelos! Though as warning making tamales is a process, a very long process. Do not be discouraged though, it also can be fun and is well worth the effort!

Equipment

  • Bamboo streamer or steamer tray for your stockpot

Ingredients

  • 5 pounds pork shoulder
  • 1 tablespoon mild olive oil or vegetable oil, plus more for coating pork
  • 4 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon kosher salt for pork filling
  • 2 tablespoon chipotle powder
  • 14 dried guajillo chiles, seeded and stemmed
  • 6 garlic cloves
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 3 cups cold water
  • 3 1/2 cups masa harina
  • 2 1/4 cups warm water
  • 10 ounces lard or vegetable shortening
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken stock or vegetable stock
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt for dough
  • 32 dried corn husks

Steps

  1. Pat the pork dry and rub it all over with olive oil. Combine 4 tablespoons of salt with the chipotle pepper and rub all over the pork. Let rest at room temperature for 1 hour.
  2. Heat the oven to 275Â°F. Place the pork in a roasting pan, cover with two layers of foil, and roast until tender and falling apart. Let the pork cool for 30 minutes.
  3. Shred the pork into strands. Discard any gristle and strain and reserve the cooking liquid.
  4. Toast the chiles in a hot skillet for about 30 seconds per side. Place in a bowl, cover with hot water, and let them soak for 30 minutes.
  5. Blend the soaked chiles, garlic, cumin, 1 teaspoon of salt, and cold water until smooth. Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pot until very hot. Add the chile puree and cook until it darkens.
  6. Stir the pork and cooking liquid into the pot. Simmer for 15 minutes. Let cool slightly before assembling the tamales.
  7. Make the tamale dough. Mix together the masa, water, then add in the lard, baking powder, stock, and salt and mix well to combine.
  8. Cover the corn husks with water. Soak until pliable, then remove from the water, separate the husks, and pat dry. Tear a few husks into 1/2-inch strips and tie a knot at the end to create ties for the tamales.
  9. Fill the tamales. Working with one husk at a time, spread 1/4 cup of the masa harina over the smooth side of the husk, leaving a 1/2-inch border. Top with 2 tablespoons of the pork, then roll the tamale and secure the bottom with one of the ties, leaving the top open.
  10. Prepare a steamer in a large stockpot partially filled with water. Stand the tamales upright in the steamer and cook until the masa pulls away from the husks, adding water as needed. Let the tamales rest in the steamer until they begin to firm up before serving.

Tips

  • Extra pulled pork filling can be used in tacos, sandwiches, enchiladas, or as a topping for a rice bowl.
  • When filling your tamales, make sure you spread the filling on the smooth side of the husk. This will allow the filling to release easily from the corn husk.
  • Store leftover tamales in a sealed container or resealable bag in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Gently reheat in a steamer.
